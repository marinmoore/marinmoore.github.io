Women's, Gender, & Sexuality Studies Research Experience

Research papers written for Women's, Gender, & Sexuality Studies courses at Dickinson College

  • Gender Markers on Identity Documents

Written for the course Immigration Politics

Researches how gender markers reinforce the hierarchical identity system that confers and denies power and privilege, including the power of the nation-state to determine which identities are registrable, legible, and therefore legitimate. Concludes that sex/gender markers should be removed from all identity documentation because the registration and regulation of gender identity contributes to unjust treatment of transgender and nonbinary immigrants.

  • Embroidery as Feminist Art, Praxis, and Resistance

Written for the course Feminist Perspectives & Theories

Investigates the history of the construction of needlework as feminine, domestic, and nonwork and of femininity as engendering needlework, domesticity, and nonwork. Examines historical uses of needlework as a liberatory means and/or a subversive art by women of color and working-class women and as a declaration of the power of femininity by activists to determine the full history evoked by embroidery’s use in feminist art.
Presented at the 2024 Dickinson College Research on Women’s, Gender, & Sexuality Symposium.

  • Minority Stress Theory and the Allostatic Load

Written for the course Reproductive Justice

Synthesizes studies on the correlation of exposure to heterosexism and rates of preterm birth for queer parents, cortisol levels of ‘out’ and ‘closeted’ queer people, queer people of color’s heightened resilience to negative health effects from exposure to heterosexism, and more. Concludes that heterosexism constitutes a reproductive injustice because it prevents queer people from living healthy lives and from raising children in a healthy and nurturing environment. However, experience coping with the stressor of heterosexism may better prepare queer people for other life stressors, like aging.
Awarded the Waidner-Spahr Library Prize for Excellence in First Year Research and presented at the 2023 Dickinson College Research on Women’s, Gender, & Sexuality Symposium.
